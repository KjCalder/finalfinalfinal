Our Vision

We live in a world struggling to hold order amoungst chaos. Peace amoungst war. Love amoungst hate. At times, all hope can be as fleeting as our bank account in this economy. But alas, we strive to be the beam that brings the world back together by helping adults, like you, finally begin using your homophones properly. One 'there, they're and their' at a time.

Brief Explainations

To keep it simple we are going to focus on 5 homophones: their, they're, there, your, and you're. Each sound the same, but they are entirely different words and proper usage is the only way you'll get any grain of respect from anyone. Including me. I notice and silently judge you everytime. It is imperative for any relationship, especially ours, to continue on a foundation of proper homophones. Let us dive into some examples:

Examples

We are going to keep it simple and straight forward:
  1. There: "Look over there!"; "I'm going there later today." (location)
  2. They're: " They're so cool!"; "They're going home right now." (they are)
    • At this point if you're struggling, don't stress it's going to be ok.
    • You are worth a lot more than just your grammar skills. I just won't be your friend anymore.
  3. Their: "Their grammar is getting so much better!" "I hope their texts look a lot better." (posession)
